Solar Proximity Cuts and the Gravitational Wave Background Signal_

Gravitational Waves
Data Analysis
Pulsar Timing

Introduction

This work focuses on a crucial data challenge in Pulsar Timing Array (PTA) observations: how to treat data collected when pulsars are observed in close angular proximity to the Sun. The main objective was to systematically investigate how removing these 'solar proximity' observations affects the inferred signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) for the Gravitational Wave Background (GWB) and its characteristic Hellings-Downs correlation.

This final investigation builds upon earlier analyses, which included applying various cuts to unfiltered data to compare against a benchmark dataset, and performing an in-depth noise analysis on data where Frequency-Dependent (FD) noise had been removed.

Analysis and Key Findings

The analysis was performed using the optimal statistic package, which estimates the significance and amplitude of spatial correlation templates, such as the Hellings-Downs (HD) curve, which is the expected quadrupolar signature of the GWB.

Using **simulated datasets**, the expected outcome was observed: as the angle cut (the amount of data excluded near the Sun) increased, the SNR for both the HD and dipolar correlations generally decreased due to the loss of data.

When examining **real datasets**, a strong correlation between the HD and Dipolar SNRs was noted, a pattern that persisted across datasets both with and without Solar Wind (SW) modelling. This highlights the complexity and sensitivity of correlation statistics to various systematic factors, including ephemeris accuracy and noise modeling.
